The best OBD2 Bluetooth scanner should be easy to use and offer various diagnostics tools. At the very least, an OBD2 scanner can check error codes and clear check engine lights. Some scanners offer advanced tools for ABS diagnostics, live data, and more.

Even if you aren’t a professional mechanic, an OBD2 code reader can be a useful tool. Not only can you diagnose and address minor car problems, but you can have a better sense of how much a car repair will cost before you take your vehicle to a mechanic.

To help you find the best OBD2 Bluetooth scanner for your needs, we recommend a variety of options – from low-cost choices to high-end scanners packed with features. Each of the above scanners works well and can be an invaluable diagnostics tool.

#1 Editor’s Choice: BlueDriver Bluetooth Pro OBDII Scan Tool

Check Price

The BlueDriver is an OBDII scan tool that provides advanced diagnostic features while still offering an easy-to-use interface that’s accessible if you aren’t a mechanic. While it has a high up-front cost (at over $100, while some other options are around $40), it doesn’t require a monthly subscription.

This scanner also topped our list of best OBD2 scanners for its utility and versatility.

Our Experience

The BlueDriver has hands-down the best app of the OBD2 Bluetooth scanners we’ve tested. The interface is intuitive – even for the novice. If you simply want to check your engine codes, this tool is a way to easily do so.

If you want to do more than check engine codes, the BlueDriver can also do that. It can monitor most vehicle systems, and you won’t need to pay a monthly fee to access the advanced monitoring features as you sometimes must with other scanners. The BlueDriver also has a Repair Report feature that suggests fixes for certain issues and fixes that technicians certified by the Automotive Center for Service Excellence verify. If you plan to use the BlueDriver mostly to maintain your car, the device is a solid OBD2 choice. It’s plug and play features make it useful for beginners and experts alike.

Buyers Guide: Best OBD2 Bluetooth Scanner

The right OBD2 Bluetooth scanner for you depends on how you plan to use it. OBD2 scanners provide a broad range of vehicle information. All modern cars have advanced computers that collect data, which can be useful for repair diagnostics if something goes wrong.

Car Compatibility

Regardless of which diagnostic scan tool you buy, make sure it’s compatible with your vehicle. Most OBD2 Bluetooth scanners will work with the most popular brands. However, if you have a foreign car, luxury vehicle, or sports car, it may not be compatible with all OBD2 scanners.

Your Mechanical Knowledge

If you have advanced mechanical know-how, an OBD2 scan tool is a necessary component for diagnosing and fixing most malfunctions. You can also use these tools to help you achieve peak performance from your car.

If you have limited car knowledge, you may only need an OBD2 scan tool to read engine codes. In this case, there’s no point spending extra for features you’ll never use.

Advanced Diagnostics

Every OBD2 Bluetooth scanner can read basic engine codes. Most also offer diagnostics for ABS, airbags, O2 sensors, and more. Some scanners require you pay a monthly fee to access these features.

Our Review Standards

To select the best OBD2 bluetooth scanners, our review team researched customer reviews from Amazon, AutoZone, Advance Auto Parts, and Walmart. We then tested each scanner, evaluating it based on the following criteria:

  • Ease of use
  • App quality
  • Data collection capabilities
  • Value

Ease Of Use

An OBD2 scanner shouldn’t be a mystery to decipher. The best scanners are usable not only by the professional mechanic. OBD2 scanners with straightforward instructions and intuitive interfaces scored best in this category.

App Quality

Every OBD2 Bluetooth scanner operates as a wireless device that connects to a smartphone or tablet. To interface with the scanner, you must launch an application. The more user-friendly the app, the better the automotive scanner.

Many OBD2 Bluetooth scanners are nearly identical, with the companion app defining the quality of the product. Scanners with useful functions and well-designed apps that don’t crash scored highest in this category.

Data Collection Capabilities

Some scanners can only read check engine lights, while others provide advanced diagnostics for ABS and SRS systems. OBD2 scanners with more features scored best in this category.

Value

Our value score is based on the relationship between a device’s scores in other categories and its total cost. An OBD2 scanner with fewer features may be a better buy for some users if it’s less expensive, especially if you don’t intend to use advanced diagnostics features and only need to clear check engine lights.

OBD2 Scanner Testing Process

We tested each of the OBD2 scanners on a 2017 Ford Fusion Hybrid. Our testers downloaded the companion app and connected the scanner to the vehicle. We tried out each of the features available. We used apps on an Android phone, though most scanners also have iOS apps.

A Bluetooth OBD2 scanner can perform the same functions as a typical OBD2 scanner. The primary difference is that Bluetooth OBD2 scanners require a smartphone or tablet to run the companion app.
